🔍 Commercial Facility No-Cooling Diagnostic

July 2025 | 3.5 Ton Split System | Complete System Recovery

📍 Initial Situation

  • Outdoor unit completely non-operational
  • Indoor temperature at 85°F during peak summer
  • No previous service documentation available
  • Critical cooling needed for facility operations

🔧 Diagnostic Process

  • Electrical testing revealed intermittent power delivery
  • Capacitor tested at 35/3 μF (rated 45/5 μF) - clear failure
  • Manual contactor test confirmed relay functionality
  • Breaker showed physical resistance indicating internal failure

📊 System Analysis

Initial Readings: • Subcooling: 13.5°F (Target: 10°F) • Superheat: 10.5°F • Suction: 163 psi | Head: 387 psi • System overcharged by ~1.8 lbs

Progressive refrigerant recovery performed in 4-6 oz increments, monitoring system response between adjustments.

Final Readings: • Subcooling: 10.2°F ✓ • Superheat: 11.8°F ✓ • Suction: 159 psi | Head: 377 psi • Supply temp: 53°F | VSAT: 56°F

💡 Technical Insights

"System was cooling under heavy load, but the overcharge was causing inefficiency and potential compressor stress. Incremental recovery allowed precise charge adjustment without overshooting targets. This methodical approach ensures longevity and efficiency."

✅ Results & Recommendations

  • Replaced failed capacitor with American-made Century Supply unit
  • Restored system to manufacturer specifications
  • Identified breaker issue - recommended licensed electrician
  • System operating at peak efficiency with documented baseline
  • Follow-up scheduled to verify charge stability

🎯 Why This Matters

This case demonstrates our commitment to systematic diagnosis over quick fixes. By taking time to properly analyze and adjust the refrigerant charge, we prevented future compressor damage and ensured optimal efficiency. The client received not just a repair, but understanding and documentation for informed decision-making.
